'''Tin(IV) chloride''', also known as '''tin tetrachloride''' or '''stannic chloride''' is a SnCl<sub>4</sub>.
At room temperature it is a colourless liquid, which fumes on contact with air, giving a stinging odour. It was first discovered by A Libavius (1550-1616) and was known as "spiritus fumans libavii" .<ref name = "Wiberg&Holleman">Egon Wiberg, Arnold Frederick Holleman (2001) ''Inorganic Chemistry'', Elsevier ISBN 0123526515</ref>
==Preparation==
It is prepared from reaction of chlorine gas with elemental tin.

==Reactions==
When mixed with a small amount of water a semi-solid crystalline mass of the pentahydrate, SnCl<sub>4</sub>.5H<sub>2</sub>O is formed.<ref name = "Wiberg&Holleman"/> This was formerly known as ''butter of tin''<ref name = "Wiberg&Holleman"/>. This compound has been shown to be best described as [SnCl<sub>4</sub>(H<sub>2</sub>O)<sub>2</sub>].3H<sub>2</sub>O, consisting of ''cis''-[SnCl<sub>4</sub>(H<sub>2</sub>O)<sub>2</sub>] units linked in chains with three hydrate water molecules.<ref>Structures of di-μ-hydroxobis[aquatrichlorotin(IV)]-1,4-dioxane(1/3), di-μ-hydroxobis[aquatrichlorotin(IV)]-1,8-epoxy-p-menthane(1/4), di-m-hydroxobis[aquatribromotin(IV)]-1,8-epoxy-p-menthane(1/4), di-μ-hydroxobis[aquatrichlorotin(IV)], and cis-diaquatetrachlorotin(IV) , Journal of the Chemical Society, Dalton Transactions: Inorganic Chemistry (1972-1999) (1980), (6), 949-53 </ref> <br />
With hydrochloric acid the complex [SnCl<sub>6</sub>]<sup>2−</sup> is formed making the so-called hexachlorostannic acid.<ref name = "Wiberg&Holleman"/><br />
Anhydrous tin(IV) chloride is a strong Lewis acid and complexes with e.g. ammonia, phosphine and phosphorus pentachloride are known.<ref name = "Wiberg&Holleman"/><br />
SnCl<sub>4</sub> is used in Friedel-Crafts reactions as a catalyst for homogeneous alkylation and cyclisation.<ref name = "Wiberg&Holleman"/><br />
With Grignard reagents tetraalkyltin compounds can be prepared<ref name = "Greenwood"></ref>:-
:SnCl<sub>4</sub> + RMgCl → SnR<sub>4</sub>

==Uses==

Stannic chloride was used as a chemical weapon in World War I.  It is also used in the glass container industry for making an external coating containing tin(IV) oxide which toughens the glass. It is a starting material for organotin compounds.

Stannic chloride is used in chemical reactions with fuming (90%) nitric acid for the selective nitration of activated aromatic rings in the presence of unactivated ones.<ref>Thurston, D. E. ''et al''. (1990). ''Synthesis'' 81&ndash;84.</ref>

'''Хлорид олова(IV)''' ('''тетрахлорид олова''', '''оловянное масло''') — SnCl<sub>4</sub>.

Тяжелая бесцветная (иногда желтоватая) жидкость удельного веса 2,28, кипит около 114&nbsp;°C, затвердевает около −33&nbsp;°C. На воздухе она дымит (алхимики её называли Spiritus fumans Libavii).

== История ==
Вещество получено немецким Андреасом Либавием в 1597 г. Позднее тетрахлорид олова назвали дымящим спиртом Либавия.

Тетрахлорид олова оказывает раздражающее действие на кожу, поэтому он использовался как Первой мировой войны.
