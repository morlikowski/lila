'''Claudio Javier López''' (born Córdoba, football striker, who currently  plays for the Kansas City Wizards in Major League Soccer. 
He is known by the nickname "El Piojo", meaning "the louse"
He is well known for his pace, dribbling skills and world class finishing.

==Early Career/Argentina==

''El Piojo'' (''The Louse'') began his professional career with Argentina in 1990 as a 16-year old.  However, he moved to Racing Club de Avellaneda the next year, where he would remain until he transferred in 1996 to Spanish club Valencia CF.

==Europe==
He remained with Valencia for five years, helping the team to be runner-up in the UEFA Champions League in the 1999-2000 season, when he was transferred to S.S. Lazio of Serie A for a sum of 35 million Euros, briefly holding the world transfer record before Hernan Crespo moved from Parma to Lazio for 53.6 million Euros<ref>[http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Football_Transfers Record Transfers]</ref>. Unfortunately, López suffered from injury problems while at Lazio, and was unable to fulfill the hopes that accompanied him.

==Mexico==

López joined Club América for the 2004 Apertura, where he played in 17 games, scoring four goals. The following season, Clausura 2005 brought better results, with López scoring a total of 14 goals overall and helping the team to its tenth League championship in its history. Claudio was instrumental to the team's success, also helping them win the CONCACAF Champions' Cup by scoring two goals in the Final over UAG Tecos.

==Racing Club==
In 2007 ''El Piojo'' returned to the Argentine Racing Club de Avellaneda, 11 years after his departure from the club, and the country.

In most of those games, usually coming in as a late sub, El Piojo made game saving goals for Racing.

==Kansas City Wizards==
On March 7th 2008, it was announced Lopez had signed with the Wizards on a free transfer.

'''Claudio Javier López''' (Río Tercero, 17 de Julho de 1974) é um futebolista argentino que joga na posição de atacante, e atualmente joga pelo Kansas City Wizards na Major League Soccer campeonato de futebol dos Estados Unidos.

''El Piojo'' (''O Piolho'') iniciou sua carreira profissional com o Estudiantes de La Plata em 1990, com apenas 16 anos. Depois, ele se transferiu para o Racing Club de Avellaneda no ano seguinte, onde ele permaneceria até que ele transferiu em 1995 ao Valencia CF.

Ele permaneceu com o Lazio da Serie A por uma quantia de 35 milhões de Euros. Infelizmente, López sofreu de problemas de lesão na Lazio, e não pôde cumprir as esperanças que o acompanharam.  
 
López foi para o Club América em 2004 no Apertura, onde ele jogou em 17 jogos, marcando quatro gols. A temporada seguinte, Clausura 2005 trouxe resultados melhores, com López marcando um total de 14 gols e ajudando o seu time ao décimo título do campeonato mexicano em sua história. Claudio era um instrumento do sucesso do time, marcando dois gols na Final em cima do UAG Tecos.

Ele teve uma carreira distinta com a seleção Argentina.  Depois de ganhar uma medalha prateada com o time sub-23 durante o 1998 e 2002.
