'''Wendy Hughes''' (born 29 July 1952 in Melbourne, Australia) is an actress.

She began her career on television in the early 1970s with appearances in ''Homicide'', ''Number 96'', ''Matlock Police'' and in 1976, ABC Mini-Series, ''Power Without Glory''.  Her big screen debut was in 1974 in the film ''Petersen''.

She then spent the rest of the 1970s and 1980s as one of the leading actresses in Australian cinema, with roles in ''Kostas'', ''My Brilliant Career'', ''Touch and Go'', ''Lonely Hearts'', ''Careful, He Might Hear You'',   ''My First Wife'', ''I Can't Get Started'', ''An Indecent Obsession'', ''Echoes of Paradise'', ''Boundaries of the Heart'', ''Warm Nights on a Slow Moving Train'' (1988), and ''Luigi's Ladies''. 

She also continued to make occasional appearances on television, such as playing Jilly Stewart in the mini-series ''Return to Eden''.
