'''Famine-33''' (, ''Holod-33'') is a 1991 film by 1932-33 Soviet-engineered famine in Ukraine, and based on the novel ''The Yellow Prince'' by Vasyl Barka.  The film is told through the lives of the Katrannyks family of six.  It relies more on images than on words shot in black-and-white.

"In an early scene, the members of an impoverished farming family solemnly take turns dipping their ladles into the single bowl of watery soup that is their only meal of the day. Later in the film, scores of villagers numb with despair and hunger huddle silently in the pouring rain outside a Government office until a truckload of armed soldiers arrives to disperse them. In the most poignant scene, a little boy who has lost his parents calls for his mother as he wonders panic- stricken through a snowy woodland where the trees are outnumbered by crosses marking the dead." <ref>Holden, Stephen. "A Family's Struggle In Stalin's Man-Made Famine: Famine-33 Film Review." ''The New York Times.'' 15 December 1993</ref>
