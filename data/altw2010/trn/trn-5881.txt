'''''Here at Last... Bee Gees... Live''''' é o primeiro concerto foi gravado em 1976 e fazia parte da turnê do álbum Children of the World, sendo o seu repertório baseado, quase que exclusivamente, nos últimos dois álbuns. Entretanto, como era esperado que a banda cantasse sucessos passados, os Bee Gees decidiram fazer um ''medley'' com algumas de suas músicas mais famosas. Essa prática foi usada pela banda até seu término e virou uma das características da banda.

Foi relançado em 1990 em dois CDs e, com a atual investida da Rhino no relançamento da obra completa dos Bee Gees, deve ser novamente relançado por volta de 2010.

'''''Here at Last... Bee Gees... Live''''' is the sixteenth album by the Bee Gees. It was released in May 1977 (see 1977 in music). It reached #8 in the USA, #8 in Australia, #1 in New Zealand and #2 in Spain, and sold 4.6 million copies worldwide.

''Here at Last'' was the first official live recording released by the Bee Gees, though many bootlegs have existed throughout the years of earlier performances. The concert was filmed and a TV special was planned, but after reviewing the footage the Bee Gees were unhappy with the quality of the video and to this day it has not been released. A single from the concert, "Edge of the Universe", was released in the U.S and the UK. which made #16 and #26, respectively. A promo single with Lonely Days live was released in the US but it didn't chart.

The album was released on CD in 1990 on a 2 CD-set, though has been out print for several years. With Rhino's current upgrade of the Bee Gees back catalog, this album should see a new release by 2009 or 2010, though Rhino has already issued remastered Bee Gees albums out of chronological order.
