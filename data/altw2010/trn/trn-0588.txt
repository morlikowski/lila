'''Will Richardson''' pedagogo estatubatuar bat da, ezaguna mundu osoan bai irakasleekin eta baita ikasleekin ere egin duen lanagatik, uler dezaten zelan erabili teknologia berriak eskoletan. Haren bloga, Weblogg-ed.com ezinbesteko erreferentzia bihurtu da hezkuntza adituen artean. Orobat, haren liburua, “Blogs, Wikis, Podcasts, and other Powerful Web Tools for Clasrooms” (Blogak, wikiak, podcastak eta sareko beste erreminta ahaltsuak ikasgelarako), oso erabilia da milaka irakasleren artean. Hogeita bi urtez ingeles irakaslea izan zen Hunterdon Central Regional High School izeneko institutuan, Flemington herrian, New Jerseyn. The New York Times egunkariak izendatu zuen “joera markatzailea irakaskuntzan”, aintzat hartuta zelan erabiltzen zituen blogak ikasgelan.

Gaur egun Connective Learning, LLC enpresa du, eta horren bidez bultzatu egiten ditu Irakurri/Idatzi teknologiak hezkuntza ertainean. Halaber, aldarrikatzen du eskolaren erreforma eta bertan teknologia berriak txertatzea. Orain dela gutxi George Lucas Hezkuntza Fundazioaren Zuzendaritza batzordekide izendatu dute.

Richardsonek zutabe bat idazten du "District Administration" aldizkarian eta beronen blogean, "The Pulse" izenekoan. Hitzaldi eta aurkezpen ugari egiten ditu munduan zehar azaltzeko zelan erabili Irakurri/Idatzi teknologiak ikasgelan.

'''Will Richardson''' is the author of the highly ranked and read edublog Weblogged<ref>[http://weblogg-ed.com/ Weblogg-ed<!-- Bot generated title -->]</ref> and author of the book, ''Blogs, Wikis, Podcasts, and other Powerful Web Tools for Classrooms''.<ref>[http://www.weblogg-ed.com/book/ Weblogg-ed » Book Excerpt in T&L Magazine<!-- Bot generated title -->]</ref>  Formerly a teacher at Hunterdon Central Regional High School in Flemington, N.J, Will was recognized for his use of blogs in the classroom as a "trendsetter in education" by the New York Times.<ref>[http://www.nytimes.com/2003/08/14/technology/circuits/14diar.html?ex=1376193600&en=eaa8f00c728408a0&ei=5007&partner=USERLAND| Can Johnny Blog?<!-- Bot generated title -->]</ref>  

Richardson is now an independent presenter and owner of Connective Learning, LLC. He promotes the implementation of Read/Write technologies in K-12 classrooms. He is also an advocate for school reform which encourages the integration of technology in learning. He was also recently named to the National Advisory Board for the [http://www.glef.org George Lucas Education Foundation].

District Administration magazine publishes a column by Richardson, who also contributes to their blog, [http://www.districtadministration.com/pulse/default.aspx The Pulse].

Richardson has been part of a panel presentation at the 2006 Milken Global Conference and has given presentations all over the world.
