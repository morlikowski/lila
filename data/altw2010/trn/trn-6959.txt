'''Lee Robert Martin''' (ur. Manchesterze United. W 2003 został kupiony przez ManU z klubu piłkarskiego Wimbledon F.C. za 1 mln funtów. W styczniu Czerwonych Diabłów. Wystąpił w 17 meczach, zdobył 1 bramkę. Mimo, że występował w nim tyko przez pół roku został wybrany najlepszym zawodnikiem tego klubu. W sierpniu tego samego roku został wypożyczony do zespołu Glasgow Rangers. Zadebiutował w spotkaniu z Celticiem Glasgow. Później jednak był dręczony przez kontuzje i zagrał tylko w 7 spotkaniach. W styczniu Stoke City. Wystapił w 13 meczach i strzelił 1 bramkę (przeciwko Southampton). Po sezonie wrócił do United i wraz z zespołem udał do tourne po Azji. Zaprezentował się ze świetnej strony strzelając przepiękną bramkę przeciwko chińskiemu Guangzhou. Trener Alex Ferguson zdecydował nie sprzedawać utalentowanego pomocnika, ponieważ ma szansę na regularne występy w Manchesterze. Jednak 5 października trener zdecydował o kolejnym wypożyczeniu Martina, tym razem do drużyny Plymouth Argyle.

'''Lee Robert Martin''' (born English footballer who currently plays as a winger for Manchester United.

He should not be confused with another Lee Martin who also played for Manchester United, as well as Celtic.

==Playing career==
Martin began his career as a trainee with Wimbledon, where he caught the eye of many Arsenal, Liverpool and Tottenham Hotspur, he signed for Manchester United on 17 December 2003, following a week-long trial in October 2003, during which time he made one appearance for the Manchester United Under-19s team. The compensation package United paid Wimbledon is estimated to be approximately £1 million, with £200k being paid up front, and the rest in instalments based on both his and Manchester United's performance.<ref></ref>

Over the remainder of the 2003&ndash;04 season, Martin made seven more appearances for United's various youth teams, and was even named as an unused substitute for the Reserves in January 2004. The following season, Martin began as a regular in the Under-18s, but he was soon promoted to the Reserve team, where he began to flourish, scoring goals as well as setting them up, including a Bolton Wanderers Reserves. The 2005&ndash;06 season was even more auspicious for Martin; after being named on the bench for two League Cup against Barnet on 26 October 2005. He was substituted after 75 minutes, allowing Darron Gibson to make his own first team debut, but he was a constant threat to the Barnet goal.

In January 2006, Martin joined United's Royal Antwerp in a loan deal until May 2006, to gain regular first team experience. During that short spell, he earned the Royal Antwerp Fans' Player of the Year award, and earned the fan-chosen Man of the Match award in five consecutive games. In the run-up to the 2006&ndash;07 season, Martin played in six of United's seven friendlies following their summer tour of Celtic. This attracted the attention of Celtic's Rangers, who sought to take him on loan for the first half of the season. Seen as not quite ready for the Premier League, Martin joined the Scottish giants with a view to an extension of the loan to the end of the season.<ref></ref>

His skills instantly impressed the Rangers support, but after a succession of injuries he failed to establish himself and returned to Manchester United in December 2006, having made just 10 appearances.<ref></ref> On Stoke City.<ref></ref> He scored his first senior goal in Stoke's 2&ndash;1 win against Southampton on 10 March 2007;<ref></ref> it was his only goal in his 14 appearances for the club.
