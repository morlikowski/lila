'''Bobby Kimball''' (lahir Orange, Toto.

== Masa kecil ==
Robert (Bobby) Troy Kimball dilahirkan di kota New Orleans, seperti Le Roux dan S.S. Fools.

== Bergabung dengan Toto ==
Kimball diterima bergabung dengan grup Toto setelah mengirim rekaman suara berisi lagu "You are the Flower" yang ditulisnya sendiri. Lagu tersebut dimasukkan ke dalam album pertama Toto. Bersama Toto, Kimball ikut serta dalam rekaman 4 album studio sebelum pecah konflik dengan anggota yang lain akibat persoalan pribadi dan obat terlarang.

Kimball dikeluarkan dari Toto sewaktu rekaman album ''Isolation'' sedang berlangsung. Selanjutnya, Kimball melanjutkan karier menyanyi secara solo di Jerman di bawah arahan produser Frank Farian yang juga mengorbitkan Milli Vanilli. Tanpa Kimball, Toto terus menerus berganti vokalis dan tidak ada yang bertahan lama.

Selama beberapa saat (1989-1990), Kimball kembali bergabung dengan Toto untuk merekam lagu-lagu dalam album kompilasi hit Toto yang diberi nama ''Past to Present 1977-1990''. Perusahaan rekaman Sony yang mengontrak Toto pada waktu itu menginginkan warna baru untuk musik Toto, sehingga Kimball kembali dikeluarkan untuk memberi tempat bagi vokalis baru Jean Michel-Byron. Tidak lama kemudian, Byron juga mengundurkan diri dan Steve Lukather menggantikan peran vokalis Toto dari tahun 1991 hingga 1999. Selama merangkap sebagai vokalis, Steve Lukather selalu dibantu vokalis tidak tetap sewaktu rekaman dan pertunjukan keliling.

== Early life==
Robert (Bobby) Troy Kimball was born in New Orleans area bands, including The Levee Band which would later beome Le Roux after Kimball left. In 1976, Bobby joined S.S. Fools in which he was the lead vocalist as well as keyboard player. That same year, the band released their self titled album.  The band broke up during it's first tour.

==Tenure in Toto==

In recorded the first four studio Isolation'', their follow-up studio album to 1982's Grammy Award-winning ''Toto IV''.<ref>[http://www.melodicrock.com/interviews/stevelukather04.html  Steve Lukather interview 2004]</ref>

==After Toto==

After leaving Toto, Bobby Kimball relocated to Germany for a solo career under Producer Frank Farian of the Far Corporation<ref>[http://www.bluedesert.dk/bobbykimball.html  Bobby Kimball Discography]</ref> (infamously noted as the creator behind ill-fated lip-sync act Milli Vanilli). He also continued to work as a session singer, singing background vocals for various artists. Bobby Kimball briefly returned to Toto during 1989-1990 to record songs for the band's greatest hits album, ''Past to Present 1977-1990'', only to be replaced by South African singer Jean Michel-Byron (a decision said to have derived from Sony, the band's record company at the time, and the label's desire to go in a new direction).<ref>[http://www.melodicrock.com/interviews/stevelukather04.html  Steve Lukather interview 2004]</ref> Byron was let go shortly thereafter, and guitarist/vocalist Steve Lukather took over Toto's primary lead vocal duties from 1991 to 1999.<ref>[http://www.melodicrock.com/interviews/stevelukather04.html  Steve Lukather interview 2004]</ref> After leaving the band again, he released a live album "Classic Toto Hits" in late 1990, in which he performed various Toto hits from over the years.<ref>[http://www.bluedesert.dk/bobbykimball.html  Bobby Kimball Discography]</ref> He recorded the album with the Frankfurt Rock Orchestra.<ref>[http://www.bluedesert.dk/bobbykimball.html  Bobby Kimball Discography]</ref> In 1994, Bobby Kimball released his first solo album "Rise Up"<ref>[http://www.bluedesert.dk/bobbykimball.html  Bobby Kimball Discography]</ref> featuring the single "Woodstock".<ref>[http://www.bluedesert.dk/bobbykimball.html  Bobby Kimball Discography]</ref>
