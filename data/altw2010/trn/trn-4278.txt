'''Hernán Büchi Buc''' (born 1949) is a Chilean economist and politician. He served as Minister of Finance under President Pinochet between 1985 and 1989.

After the recession of the early 1980s, Büchi's appointment as Finance Minister in 1985:

<blockquote>"...marked the beginning of economic recovery. Büchi's strategy was to create the financial conditions for stable, export-led growth and to reorganize the productive structures of the export sector. Control of public spending, periodic devaluations, and incentives for domestic savings, foreign investment and the repatriation of capital gradually brought inflation down to 12 per cent by 1989, the lowest rate in Latin America. A vigorous campaign to sell parcels of the public debt to private investors in exchange for shares in Chilean industries reduced the nation's debt burden by over $4 billion...Economic growth averaged between 5 per cent and 6 per cent in 1985-8, the highest rate in the region".<ref>Edwin Williamson, ''The Penguin History of Latin America'' (Penguin Books, 1992), pp. 508-9.</ref></blockquote>

In the 1989 Chilean presidential election Büchi stood for the centre-right Democracy and Progress Party. Büchi's campaign hired a former public relations adviser to former British Prime Minister Timothy Bell.<ref>Andy Beckett, ''Pinochet in Piccadilly. Britain and Chile's Hidden History'' (Faber and Faber, 2002), p. 217.</ref> Büchi came second with 2,051,975 votes (29.40%). However he "received more support from women than men in 59 of the 60 electoral districts, the exception being in southernmost district of Magallanes, where his support was about equal between the sexes".<ref>[http://journals.cambridge.org/action/displayAbstract?fromPage=online&aid=259965 Paul H. Lewis, 'The 'Gender Gap' in Chile', ''Journal of Latin American Studies'' (2004), 36: 719-742.]</ref>

'''Hernán Alberto Büchi Buch''' (* chilien, ministre des finances de 1985 à 1989 et candidat de la droite aux élections présidentielles du 14 décembre 1989. 

Fils d'un officier de marine, le jeune Hernán Büchi est pourtant sympathisant communiste dans les années 60. Sa famille l'envoie étudier aux États-Unis où il devient balayeur pour payer ses études à l'université de Columbia, qu'il réussit brillamment. 

Converti au capitalisme et au libéralisme économique, il rentre au Chili en 1975, alors gouverné par la junte militaire dirigée par le général Pinochet. Il devient conseiller économique du ministre de l'économie, Pablo Baraona et directeur adjoint de la compagnie nationale du téléphone.

A partir de 1979, il travaille avec José Piñera, le ministre du travail, à la privatisation du système des retraites. 

Il occupe diverses autres fonctions importantes dans l'administration avant d'être nommé ministre de la Propriété le 12 février 1985.
