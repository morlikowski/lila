Das '''Crosby County''' ist ein County im US-Bundesstaat Vereinigten Staaten. Im Jahr 2000 hatte das County 7072 Einwohner und eine Bevölkerungsdichte von 3 Einwohner / km². Der Sitz der County-Verwaltung ist in Crosbyton. Das County gehört zu den Dry Countys, was bedeutet, dass der Verkauf von Alkohol eingeschränkt oder verboten ist.

==Geographie==
Das County liegt nordwestlich des geographischen Zentrums von Texas und hat eine Fläche von 2335 km², wovon 6 km² Wasserfläche ist. Das County grenzt im Uhrzeigersinn an die Countys: Floyd County, Dickens County, Garza County und Lubbock County.

==Geschichte==
Crosby County wurde 1876 aus Teilen des Bexar County und Young County gebildet. Benannt wurde es nach Stephen Crosby, einem Polizeipräsidenten von Texas.

==Demografische Daten==
Nach der Volkszählung im Jahr 2000 lebten hier 7.072 Menschen in 2.512 Haushalten und 1.866 Familien. Die Bevölkerungsdichte betrug 3 Einwohner pro km². Ethnisch betrachtet setzte sich die Bevölkerung zusammen aus 63,77% weißer Bevölkerung, 3,89% Afroamerikanern, 0,54% amerikanischen Ureinwohnern, 0,03% Asiaten, 0,07% Bewohnern aus dem pazifischen Inselraum und 29,89% aus anderen ethnischen Gruppen. 1,81% stammten von zwei oder mehr ethnischen Gruppen ab. 48,93% der Einwohner waren spanischer oder latein-amerikanischer Abstammung.

'''Crosby County''' is a seat of the county is Crosbyton. The county and its seat are both named for dry, counties in the state of Texas. It forms part of the Lubbock metropolitan area.

==Geography==
According to the U.S. Census Bureau, the county has a total area of 902&nbsp;square miles (2,335&nbsp;km²), of which, 900&nbsp;square miles (2,330&nbsp;km²) of it is land and 2&nbsp;square miles (5&nbsp;km²) of it (0.24%) is water.

==Demographics==
As of the White, 3.89% Black or African American, 0.54% Native American, 0.03% Asian, 0.07% Pacific Islander, 29.89% from other races, and 1.81% from two or more races.  48.93% of the population were Hispanic or Latino of any race.

There were 2,512 households out of which 35.6% had children under the age of 18 living with them, 59.0% were married couples living together, 11.4% had a female householder with no husband present, and 25.7% were non-families. 23.8% of all households were made up of individuals and 13.4% had someone living alone who was 65 years of age or older.  The average household size was 2.78 and the average family size was 3.30.

In the county, the population was spread out with 30.7% under the age of 18, 8.5% from 18 to 24, 24.0% from 25 to 44, 21.1% from 45 to 64, and 15.6% who were 65 years of age or older.  The median age was 34 years. For every 100 females there were 91.1 males.  For every 100 females age 18 and over, there were 87.2 males.

The median income for a household in the county was $25,769, and the median income for a family was $29,891. Males had a median income of $23,775 versus $17,229 for females. The per capita income for the county was $14,445.  About 22.6% of families and 28.1% of the population were below the poverty line, including 36.6% of those under age 18 and 22.7% of those age 65 or over.
