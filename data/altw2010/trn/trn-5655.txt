'''''Who's That Girl''''' is a soundtrack album to the film of the same name starring American singer-songwriter Madonna, who also performs four of the album's nine songs. It was released on Who's That Girl''. Unlike the flop film, upon release, it was a major success, being certified Gold & Platinum in the U.S on July 29, 1987, denoting 1 million shipments there & selling in excess of 5 million copies worldwide.<ref name="w4m">[http://www.world4madonna.com/charts/ Madonna charts and sale figures]</ref>

==Album history==
Madonna contributed four songs to the soundtrack, continuing her work with title track peaking at number one in the UK, The Look of Love," which was released outside of UK. 

Although ''Who's That Girl'' is generally regarded as a Madonna album, as she was the main performer, the soundtrack also features five other music acts. In fact, Duncan Faure's "24 Hours" and Michael Davidson's "Turn It Up" were also released as singles. Madonna later performed "Who's That Girl," "Causing a Commotion," and "The Look of Love" on her 1987 ''Who's That Girl Tour.''  In 1990, she performed "Causing a Commotion" for her ''Blond Ambition Tour''. She has not performed material from this album since.

'''''Who's That Girl - Original Motion Picture Soundtrack''''' è un album che contiene la colonna sonora del film ''Who's that girl''. È stato pubblicato il 21 luglio 1987 per l'etichetta discografica Sire Records.

==Il disco==
Nell'album sono contenute quattro canzoni della cantante Madonna che è anche la protagonista del film: i tre singoli ''Who's That Girl'', ''USA.

Le canzoni di Madonna contenute nell'album furono presentate dal vivo nell'''stadio comunale di Firenze e allo stadio comunale di Torino. Il concerto di quest'ultima data fu trasmesso in diretta su Raiuno e Madonna ne realizzò un VHS dal titolo ''Ciao Italia''. Nell'occasione la popstar incontrò alcuni suoi lontani parenti italiani che risiedevano nella città di origine del padre.
