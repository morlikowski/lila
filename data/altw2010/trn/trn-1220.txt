'''Феликс Филип Каниц''' е австро-унгарски еврейски прозход<ref>http://www.bnr.bg/RadioBulgaria/Emission_Bulgarian/Theme_IstoriaIReligia/Material/2705Kanits.htm</ref>. 

Записва се във Виенския университет, когато е едва на 17 години. Там учи южните славяни. 

Автор е на няколко научни труда, сред които ''Дунавска България и Балканът'' (''Donau-Bulgarien und der Balkan''), ''Кралство Сърбия земља и сръбския народ от Римско време до XIX век'' (нем. Das Konigreich Serbien und das Serbenvolk von der Romerzeit bis dur Gegenwart, 1904) и други.

'''Felix Philipp Kanitz''' (Bulgarian and Serbian Austro-Hungarian naturalist, geographer, ethnographer, archaeologist and author of travel notes.
right|thumb|300px|A drawing of the first factory in the Balkans (opened in 1833 in [[Sliven, modern Bulgaria) by Felix Kanitz]]

Kanitz was born in Budapest to a rich Jewish family and enrolled in art in the University of Vienna in 1846, at the age of seventeen.<ref name=jewenc> {{cite web
| url = http://www.jewishencyclopedia.com/view.jsp?artid=87&letter=K
| title = Jewish Encyclopedia: KANITZ, FELIX PHILIPP
| last = Singer
| first = Isidore
| coauthors = Ferederick T. Haneman
| accessdate = 2006-06-05
}}</ref> He travelled extensively after Bosnia, Macedonia. He worked on the topic until 1889, the knowledge he gathered being characterized as particularly important for the period. A good painter and drawer, Kanitz was also the author of a number of black and white drawings related to the life in the Balkans. Born a Jew, he later converted to Christianity.<ref name=jewenc/>

Between 1870 and 1874 he was the first custodian of the Anthropologisch-Urgeschichtliches Museum in Vienna.<ref>{{cite web
| url = http://www.nhm-wien.ac.at/ag/Ziele.htm
| title = Geschichte und Ziele der Anthropologischen Gesellschaft in Wien
| language = German
| accessdate = 2006-06-05
}}</ref> He died in the Austrian capital on 8 January 1904.

Kanitz is regarded as one of the first profound ethnographers of the South Slavs. As such, he has earned great respect particularly in modern Serbia and Bulgaria. A village in Vidin Province in northwestern Bulgaria is named after him.<ref>{{cite web
