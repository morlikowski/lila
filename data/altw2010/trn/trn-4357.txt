'''Greip''' ( ''greyp)'' or '''Saturn LI''' (provisional designation '''S/2006 S 4''') is a Saturn. Its discovery was announced by Scott S. Sheppard, David C. Jewitt, Jan Kleyna, and Brian G. Marsden on June 26, 2006, from observations taken between January 5 and May 1, 2006.
S/2006 S 4 is about 6 kilometres in diameter, and orbits Saturn at an average distance of 18,066 Mm in 906.556 days, at an retrograde direction and with an eccentricity of 0.3735.

It is named after Greip, a giantess in Norse mythology.
