'''''Ghost Dad''''' is a 1990 widower's spirit is able to communicate with his children after his death. It was critically eviscerated, and wound up on many critics' "Worst of 1990" and "Worst of all time" lists.

==Plot==
Elliot Hopper (Bill Cosby) is a recently widowed father of three who has nearly bankrupted the family in attempts to save his lingering wife. Elliot is working on a business deal to get the family out of debt when he climbs into the taxicab of a maniacal Satanist.

In an attempt to end the ensuing mania, Elliot proclaims that he is in fact Satan and orders the driver to stop the cab immediately. Overwrought with the confrontation, the driver releases the steering wheel. The taxi slams into the barrier of a bridge and teeters over a river. As Elliot frantically tries to exit, the vehicle topples into the river, an incident which appears to kill both occupants.

Rather than awakening to a scientist studying the paranormal who agrees to "send [him] back".

Elliot, who had neglected to get floating spirit that can only be seen in darkened rooms, Elliot returns to his house and concocts a scheme to somehow finish his business deal and be approved for health coverage before his body is discovered and news of his death is made public.  His children are in on the plan and try their best to keep their father's secret and help him commit insurance fraud from beyond the grave.

==Critical reaction==
Since its release, ''Ghost Dad'' has been universally ravaged by critics and has a 0% rating on movie rating website ''RottenTomatoes.com''.<ref>[http://www.rottentomatoes.com/m/ghost_dad/ Rotten Tomatoes ''Ghost Dad'' reviews], June 1, 2007</ref>

How did a production executive go for it? Who ever thought this was a good idea?

A stunning performance by the seven-year-old Michael Rathbun is quite possibly the only saving grace that this film offers. <ref>[http://rogerebert.suntimes.com/apps/pbcs.dll/article?AID=/19900629/REVIEWS/6290301/1023 RogerEbert.com Chicago Sun-Times movie reviews], June 1, 2007</ref>
</blockquote>

'''Ghost Dad – Nachrichten von Dad''' ist eine US-amerikanische Filmkomödie von Sidney Poitier aus dem Jahr 1990.

== Handlung ==

Der Witwer Elliot Hopper hat keine Zeit für seine Kinder, er gilt als ein Workaholic. Hopper besteigt ein Taxi, der Fahrer rast und das Taxi kommt von der Straße ab, stürzt in den Abgrund. Hopper schleppt sich zurück auf die Straße. Zu seiner Bestürzung muss er feststellen, dass er für die meisten Menschen nicht mehr sichtbar ist - er ist zum Geist geworden. Schon wieder gerät er in Stress: Seine Zeit als Geist ist beschränkt und er muss die Zukunft seiner Kinder sichern. Die Geisterexistenz hat allerdings auch Vorteile: Er kann durch Telefonleitungen schlüpfen, sich auf Lampenschirme setzen und hat viele andere nützliche Fähigkeiten, die den Lebenden nicht zur Verfügung stehen. Zum Schluss kommt alles anders als gedacht: Mit Hilfe eines Geister-Arztes und seiner Kinder kann der geplagte Vater doch wieder in seine sterbliche Hülle schlüpfen und sein Leben wie vor dem Unfall fortsetzen.
