'''لونغتوك غياتسو''' (1806 م – 1815 م) الدالاي لاما التاسع، ولد في دان شوخور قرية صغيرة في خام لأبويه تينزين شويكيونغ و دهوندوب دولما. عام 1807 تقرر بأنه الدالاي لاما التاسع وأخذ إلى لاسا في موكب كبير، وفي عام 1810 توج في قصر بوتالا. تقدم بنذور الراهب المبتدئ وأُعطي اسم لونغتوك غياتسو. توفي عام 1815 وهو صغير في التاسعة.

'''Lungtok Gyatso''' (1806 &ndash; 1815), also spelled '''Lungtog Gyatso''' and '''Luntok Gyatso''', was the 9th Dalai Lama of Tibet.

Lungtok Gyatso was born in 1805 or 1806 near Choekor Monastery in Dan Chokhor, a small village in the historical region of Kham to Tenzin Choekyong and Dhondup Dolma.<ref>"The Ninth Dalai Lama LUNGTOK GYATSO." [http://namgyalmonastery.org/hhdl/hhdl9]</ref>  
 
In 1807, he was recognized as the reincarnation of the Panchen Lama, who gave him the name Lungtok Gyatso. This same year the elderly Regent, Ta-task Nga-wang Gon-po passed away and the De-mo Tul-ku Nga-wang Lo-zang Tub-ten Jig-me Gya-tso (d. 1819) was appointed to replace him.<ref>Khetsun Sangpo Rinpoche. (1982). "Life and times of the Eighth to Twelfth Dalai Lamas." ''The Tibet Journal''. Vol. VII Nos. 1 & 2. Spring/Summer 1982, p. 48.</ref>

:"The English explorer Thomas Manning, who reached Lhasa in 1812, described his meeting with the 9th Dalai Lama, who was seven years old at the time, in rhapsodic terms. 'The lama's beautiful and interesting face engrossed all my attention,' Manning wrote. 'He had the simple, unaffected manners of a well-educated princely child. His face was, I thought, affectingly beautiful. He was of a gay and cheerful disposition. I was extremely affected by this interview with the lama. I could have wept through strangeness of sensation.'"<ref>Brown, Mick. ''The Dance of 17 Lives: The Incredible True Story of Tibet's 17th Karmapa'', pp. 28-29. (2004) Bloomsbury Publishing, New York, N.Y. ISBN 1-58234-177-X.</ref> 

Unfortunately, he served only briefly owing to his death at the age of nine in 1815. "The entire nation was plunged into sorrow", which lasted until the recognition of the new reincarnation eight years later.<ref>Khetsun Sangpo Rinpoche. (1982). "Life and times of the Eighth to Twelfth Dalai Lamas." ''The Tibet Journal''. Vol. VII Nos. 1 & 2. Spring/Summer 1982, p. 49.</ref>
