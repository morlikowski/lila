'''Nereid''' on Neptunuksen kolmanneksi suurin kuu, läpimitaltaan 340 km. Nereidin löysi Gerard P. Kuiper v. 1949. Sitä pidettiin pitkään Neptunuksen uloimpana kuuna kunnes vuosina 2002 ja 2003 löytyi 5 uutta kuuta jotka kiertävät vielä kauempana. Nereidin kiertorata on epäkeskisempi kuin millään muulla tunnetulla kuulla Aurinkokunnassa, vaihdellen 1 353 600 kilometrista 9 623 700 kilometriin. Tämä viittaa siihen että se saattaa olla Neptunuksen kaappaama Kuiperin vyöhykkeen kappale.

Nereid oli liian kaukana Voyager 2-avaruusluotaimen Neptunuksen ohilennon aikana 1989 jotta siitä olisi saatu tarkkoja kuvia. Kuvat paljastavat vain Nereidin epäsäännöllisen muodon.

'''Nereid''' ( [UK, US], or  [UK]; Greek ''Νηρηΐδα),'' or '''Neptune&nbsp;II''', is a moon of Neptune.

Nereid was discovered on 1 May 1949 by Gerard P. Kuiper, who proposed the name in the report of his discovery. It is named after the Nereids, sea-nymphs of Greek mythology. 

Nereid was the outermost known moon of Neptune from its discovery until 2002. Nereid's diameter is 340 km, making it the third-largest moon in the Neptune system. Its eccentric and varies from 1,372,000 to 9,655,000 kilometres. This is the highest mean eccentricity of any known satellite in the Bestla and Margaret, because of the Kozai mechanism.<ref name="IAU_MPC_NSES"><br>As of 2008, the Natural Satellites Ephemeris Service lists the current instantaneous eccentricities and epochs as:<br>
Margaret: 0.7979 (2006 Mar. 6.0 TT = JDT 2453800.5)<br>
Nereid: 0.7464 (2007 Oct. 27.0 TT = JDT 2454400.5)<br>
Bestla: 0.7309 (2007 Apr. 10.0 TT = JDT 2454200.5)<br>
(To use this utility: pick a gas giant, deselect Ephemerides and select Orbital Elements)</ref> The unusual orbit suggests that it may be a captured Triton.

Halimede may be a fragment of Nereid that was broken off during a collision.<ref name="Grav2004U">T. Grav, M. J. Holman and W. Fraser, ''Photometry of Irregular Satellites of Uranus and Neptune'', The Astrophysical Journal, '''613''' (2004), pp. L77–L80. [http://arxiv.org/abs/astro-ph/0405605 Preprint]</ref>

Nereid was too far away from ''Voyager 2'' to be properly imaged when the spacecraft visited the Neptune system in 1989. Photos sent back show only its highly irregular shape, and no surface features could be seen at the resolution available.
