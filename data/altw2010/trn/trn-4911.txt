'''Sand knifefishes''' are freshwater fishes of the '''Rhamphichthyidae''' family, from genera and around 15 species in this group.<ref name=Nelson></ref>

Just like most part of the members of the Gymnotiformes group, they also have elongated and compressed bodies and electric organs. The long anal fin actuall extends from before the pectoral fins to the tip of the tail. There is no dorsal fin.<ref name=Fishbase></ref> Teeth are absent in the oral jaws and the snout is very long and tubular. The nostrils are very close together. This group is sometimes known as the tubesnout knifefishes for this reason.<ref name=Nelson/>

They are nocturnal and burrow in the sand during the day.<ref name=Fishbase/>
