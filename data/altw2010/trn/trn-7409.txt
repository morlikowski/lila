'''Мексиканская черепаха''' (Dermatemys mawii) - крупное животное, с овальным панцирем длиной до 40 см. Голова небольшая, с заострённой вытянутой мордой, может полностью втягиваться под панцирь. Лапы снабжены плавательными перепонками. Эта черепаха обитает в реках и озёрах Восточной Мексики, Гватемалы и Гондураса. В Гватемале её ловят и употребляют в пищу.

The '''Mesoamerican River Turtle''' (''Dermatemys mawii'') locally known as the "hickatee" or "tortuga blanca"-(''white turtle'') is the only family Dermatemydidae. It is a nocturnal, aquatic turtle that lives in larger rivers and lakes in Central America, from southern Mexico to northern Honduras. It is considered an endangered species. 

== Description ==

''Dermatemys'' species are fairly large turtles, attaining a maximum size of 65&nbsp;cm (25&nbsp;inches) in carapace length, and they can weigh in the range of 20&nbsp;kg (44&nbsp;lbs).

They have a flattened carapace, that is usually a solid grey or almost black in color. Their plastron is normally cream colored. In juveniles, a distinctive keel is found down the center of the carapace, and the outer edges have serrations. These features are lost as the turtle ages. Its skin is predominantly the same color as the shell, with redish or peach colored markings around the neck and underside. Males can be differentiated from females by yellow markings on either side of their head, and a longer, thicker tail.

== Reproduction ==

Mating and nesting occurs in the fall, from September to November. Females lay clutches of 6-20 eggs on the banks of waterways that they otherwise would not normally be able to reach without the flooding caused by the seasonal rainfall.
