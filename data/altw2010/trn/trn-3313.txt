Der '''Höckerkopfgecko''' (''Rhacodactylus auriculatus'') gehört zu der Familie der Doppelfingergeckos und wird nur im südlichen Teil der Insel Neukaledonien gefunden. Zuerst beschrieben wurde ''Rhacodactylus auriculatus'' von Bavay 1869. Das Habitat der Geckos wird durch Abholzung bedroht, stehen aber leider trotzdem nicht unter dem Washingtoner Artenschutz-Übereinkommen.

Der deutsche Name von ''R. auriculatus'' leitet sich durch die 2 Höcker auf dem Kopf bzw. über den Augen her. 
Andere charakteristische Merkmale sind z.B. ein dünner Schwanz, sowie Milbentaschen.

Höckerkopfgeckos sind Baumbewohner, sind aber auch oft am Boden aufzufinden, vor allem während der Eiablage.
Die Tiere legen 2 Eier pro Ablage und Jungtiere schlüpfen nach 60-90 Tagen inkubationszeit.

'''New Caledonian bumpy gecko''' or '''gargoyle gecko''', ''Rhacodactylus auriculatus'', is a habitat is threatened by deforestation on the island. This gecko, along with several other ''Rhacodactylus'' species are being considered for protective measures by CITES, which would put restrictions on their exportation. This gecko was first described by Bavay in 1869.

== Description ==
More commonly known as the gargoyle gecko, this reptile gets its name from the cranial bumps that give the appearance of horns or ears. Other characteristics of this gecko include a thin tail, which will regenerate if it drops off, thin toe pads, and mite pockets found on the rear legs. 

They occur in many colors, including varying shades of captive bred for particular traits.

== Behavior ==
Gargoyle geckos are nocturnal and eggs per clutch which hatch 60-90 days after they are laid.

== In captivity ==
Gargoyle geckos make interesting crickets and waxworms. They also will accept fruit mashes and fruit baby food. Males should be housed separately or as a part of a breeding pair or trio with females. Males housed together will often fight. Females are social and can be housed together. Young geckos should also be housed apart because they can be aggressive toward their cage mates.
