"'''Summer '68'''" é uma solo de trompete no meio e final da música. Diferente da inspiração clássica em Sysyphus no Ummagumma, Summer '68 mostra o retorno de Richard Wright à uma composição lírica ao invés da instrumental.

A canção também mostra o tédio de excursar e o desejo de fugir de tudo : "My friends are lying in the sun, I wish that I was there..."

"'''Summer '68'''" is a song by Richard Wright, it is about a one night stand, likely with a groupie, and the return to habitual life<ref>[http://www.songfacts.com/detail.php?id=4016 Summer '68 by Pink Floyd Songfacts<!-- Bot generated title -->]</ref>. The soft piano intro contrasts with the bombastic trumpet solo breaks in the middle and end of the song, the latter breaks echoing the "Father's Shout" theme from the "Atom Heart Mother" suite. Unlike the Classical-inspired Sysyphus on Ummagumma, "Summer '68" sees Wright returning to lyrical rather than instrumental song writing. 

The song also hints at the tedium of touring and a desire to escape from it all: 'My friends are lying in the sun, I wish that I was there...'
