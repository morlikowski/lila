'''BMW Sauber F1.08''' on Formula 1 -auto, jota BMW Sauber -talli käyttää kaudella . Auto julkistettiin 14. tammikuuta 2008 Münchenissä Saksassa. Autoa kilpailuissa ajavat Nick Heidfeld ja Robert Kubica.<ref name="Julkistus"></ref>

Edelliskaudella valmistajien MM-sarjassa toiseksi sijoittunut talli haluaa uudella autollaan haastaa kärkitallit Ferrarin ja McLarenin.<ref name="Julkistus" /> BMW toivoo myös, että uudella autolla se pystyisi nousemaan voittajatalliksi nyt kolmannella kaudelaan F1:sissä.<ref name="munanimentää"></ref>

Australian GP:stä autolla saavutettiin toinen sija ja keskeytys, kun Heidfeld ajoi toiseksi ja Kubica keskeytti Malesian GP:ssä Kubica ajoi autolla toiseksi ja Heidfeld kuudenneksi. The '''BMW Sauber F1.08''' is a Formula One racing car used by the BMW Sauber team during the 2008 Formula One season.

==Launch==
left|thumb|250px|[[Robert Kubica driving the F1.08 at the Early 2008 Testing at Valencia, 01-23-2008.]]The F1.08 was launched on January 14 2008 in Munich.<ref name="BMW presents new car in Munich"></ref> The car was taken out for the first time at the Valencia circuit a day after the launch, where Nick Heidfeld took the car for a brief spin at BMW Welt, the BMW delivery center in Munich. The first track outing of the F1.08 was given to Robert Kubica.<ref name="Positive start to BMW Sauber F1.08 testing at Valencia"></ref>

===Designer change===
This is the first BMW Sauber to not be designed by Honda F1 in January 2008.<ref>[http://news.bbc.co.uk/sport1/hi/motorsport/formula_one/6289334.stm BBC SPORT | Motorsport | Formula One | New designer for struggling Honda<!-- Bot generated title -->]</ref>

===Livery and sponsorship===
For the 2008 season, BMW Sauber has kept the white and navy livery used on its cars in 2006 and 2007. However, O2 has been replaced by T-Systems <ref name="BMW Sauber F1 Team and T-Systems launch technology partnership"></ref>, and Cadence Design Systems<ref name="BMW Sauber F1 Team welcomes Cadence as official equipment supplier"></ref> have also became an official equipment supplier. The team continues to be sponsored and supported by Petronas, Intel, Dell and Credit Suisse.

==Technical developments of the F1.08==
===Rules changes===
The FIA have implemented a set of rules for the 2008 season that will see all teams use one gearbox for four races and a standard McLaren Electronic Systems ECU that prevents the use of driving aids such as traction control and engine braking.

===Aerodynamic and mechanical changes===
The sidepod areas have undergone massive changes for 2008, along with a cleaning up of the area in front of the rear wing.<ref name="F1.08: Tiny rear and and new layout of T-wings"></ref> This makes the airflow cleaner and smoother over the car to the rear wing,<ref name="Shaping up the sidepod: BMW Sauber F1.08"></ref> increasing downforce. The F1.08 also introduces a new front wing that is suspended above the nose.<ref name="BMW Sauber learn from McLaren nose"></ref> It is similar to the wing introduced on the McLaren MP4-22 in 2007, but it instead has a triple profile. The team has also tested 'antlers': vertical fins on the nosecone of the F1.08 in testing.

==2008 season==

thumb|left|[[Nick Heidfeld gave the F1.08 a podium finish on its début race meeting by finishing second at the 2008 Australian Grand Prix.]]

===Early 2008 testing===
The first run of the F1.08 was at an exclusive three day test at the Valencia circuit in Spain. Heidfeld had the duty of testing the F1.08 whilst teammate Kubica drove the F1.07B for the duration of the three day test with the exception of the first day where Heidfeld drove the F1.07B.<ref name="BMW Sauber wrap up exclusive Valencia test"></ref> However when the team returned to the same circuit to test the F1.08 on 23 January, it was shown to be slow in comparison to its competitors and the team's previous pace.<ref name="Valencia day two - Ferrari dominate in Spain"></ref> Technical director Willy Rampf has played down rumors of a lack of speed<ref name="BMW working on slow F1.08 - Rampf"></ref> and has confirmed that the car will be running in a revised form at the Barcelona test on 1 to 3 February 2008.

===Successful start to season===
The BMW Sauber team enjoyed a successful start to the 2008 season with Nick Heidfeld finishing second in Melbourne, 5.4 seconds behind the McLaren of Lewis Hamilton. At the next race in Malaysia Robert Kubica finished second, 19.5 seconds behind Kimi Räikkönen and his Ferrari; Nick Heidfeld also took the fastest lap. At the Bahrain Grand Prix Kubica took pole, just 0.027 seconds ahead of Ferrari driver Felipe Massa. In the race, however, Kubica started badly and lost ground to Massa in the first corner and to Raikkonen early in the race as well. Kubica and Heidfeld finished behind both Ferarris, in 3rd and 4th  places, respectively.

After the Bahrain race BMW Sauber were leading the Constructors' Championship, a point ahead of Ferrari and two ahead of McLaren-Merecedes.
