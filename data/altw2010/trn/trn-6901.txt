'''Mivtahim''' (, lit. ''Safe Havens'') is a moshav in southern Israel. Located in the Hevel Eshkol area of the north-western Negev desert near the Gaza Strip border and covering an area of 4,000 dunams, it falls under the jurisdiction of Eshkol Regional Council. In 2006 it had a population of 335.

The settlement was first established on 7 January 1947 as a kibbutz of the Isaiah 32:18;
<blockquote>And my people shall abide in a peaceable habitation, and in '''secure dwellings''', and in quiet resting-places.<ref>[http://www.mechon-mamre.org/p/pt/pt1032.htm Isaiah Chapter 32] Mechon Mamre</ref></blockquote>

On 22 April 1948 there was a severe clash between the British army and guards of the kibbutz, with armed units including tanks arriving at the gates. During the 1948 Arab-Israeli War residents decided to evactuate Mivtahim and join with residents of Nitzanim to found a new kibbutz. All that remains today of the original settlement is a security building.
