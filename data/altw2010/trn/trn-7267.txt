'''Leonard Stephen Pascoe''' (born Test and ODI cricketer.

Pascoe played in 14 Tests and 29 ODIs between 1977 and 1982, during which time he transferred to 82 Frank Worrell Trophy series in Australia.

Geoff Lawson spoke in his autobiography ''Henry'' of how the fast-bowler Pascoe, the son of Macedonian<ref>Peter,Hill. (1989) The Macedonians in Australia, Hesperian Press, Carlisle, pp.131</ref> immigrants, was subject to baiting about his origins, especially from the Chappell brothers. He once famously stated that "a tiger never changes its spots."

'''Leonard Stephen Pascoe''' (né le 13 février 1950), communément appelé '''Len Pascoe''', est un ancien joueur de cricket test et son premier équipe d'Australie en 1977. Il jouait en particulier en tant que fast bowler.

Né de parents immigrants yougoslaves, il est né sous le nom de Durtanovich.
Il a fait partie des joueurs qui ont rejoint la World Series Cricket, ce qui l'a empêché d'avoir une carrière internationale plus riche.
