'''Твер''' (у Російській Федерації, центр Тверської області, порт на Волзі, біля гирла річки Тверци.  Населення 405,6 тис. осіб. (2006), територія більше 147 км², включає ряд природних лісопарків: Комсомольський, Певомайський, Бобачевський, Березовий Гай і Сахаровський парк (у 1982 оголошені державними заповідниками).  Місто розташоване на берегах річок Тверца, Тьмака, Лазур, Сомінка, за 167 км від Москви і за 485 км від  станція на залізниці  Москва — Санкт-Петербург.

Клімат помірно-континентальний: середня температура січня −9,6 °C, липня +18,2 °C, середня річна кількість опадів — 593 мм.

== Історія == 

За архівними даними поселення на мису при впаданні Тьмаки в Волгу існувало вже в IX—X вв. На думку історика В. А. Кучкіна, фортеця в Твері споруджена в 30-40-х рр. XII століття в ході боротьби ростовсько-суздальських князів з Тверського кремля, датуються кінцем XII — початком XIII століть.

== Економіка == 

Твер виробляє 40 % промисловій продукції області.  Основні галузі — легка, хімічна і машинобудівна промисловість. Крупні підприємства: комбінати — бавовняно-паперовий, штучного волокна, камвольний, штучної шкіри; заводи — вагонобудівний (пасажирські вагони), екскаваторний, машинобудівний (мотовози), електроапаратурний. Працює 2 великих поліграфічних комбінату, є підприємства будматеріалів і харчової (м'ясокомбінат) промисловості.

'''Tver''' () is a city in 2002 Census).  Tver was formerly the capital of a powerful medieval state and a model provincial town in Imperial Russia with population of 60,000 on
January 1/14.1913. It is located at the confluence of the Volga and Tvertsa Rivers. The city was known as '''Kalinin''' () from 1931 to 1990.

==Medieval origins==
thumb|right|200px|View of Tver in the winter
The first written record of Tver is dated 1164. Originally a minor settlement of Novgorodian traders, it passed to the Grand Prince of Vladimir in 1209. In 1246, Alexander Nevsky granted it to his younger brother Yaroslav Yaroslavich (d.&nbsp;1271), from whom a dynasty of local princes descended. Four of them were killed by the Golden Horde and were proclaimed saints by the Russian Orthodox church.

Formerly a land of woods and bogs, the Tver principality was quickly transformed into one of the richest and most populous Russian states. As the area was hardly accessible for Tatar raids, there was a great influx of population from the recently devastated South. By the end of the century, it was ready to vie with Moscow for supremacy in Russia. Both Tver and Moscow were young cities, so the outcome of their rivalry was far from being certain.

==Grand princedom==
thumb|200px|right|17th century icon of St.&nbsp;[[Mikhail Yaroslavich|Michael of Tver, slain by the Mongols, holding the town of Tver in his hands.]]

Mikhail of Tver, who ascended the throne of alliance with the mighty Grand Duchy of Lithuania, managed to raise Tver's prestige even higher.

Exasperated by Dmitry's influence, Prince Moscow engineered his murder by the Mongols in 1326. On hearing the news of this crime, the city revolted against the Horde. The latter joined its forces with Muscovites and brutally repressed the rebellion. Many citizens were killed, enslaved, or deported. This was the fatal blow to Tver's pretensions for supremacy in Russia.

In the second half of the 14th century, Tver was further weakened by Kremlin court.

==Later history==
At last, on Ivan the Great seized the city. The principality was given as an appanage to Ivan's grandson, only to be abolished several decades later. Last scions of the ruling dynasty were executed by Ivan the Terrible during the village of Kushalino, 28&nbsp;km north-east of Tver.

thumb|right|200px|Catherine Palacethumb|right|200px|Statue of Lenin on the Central square in Tver

The city's decline was not irrevocable, however. With the foundation of St.&nbsp;Petersburg, Tver gained importance as a principal station on the highway (and later railway) from Moscow to St.&nbsp;Petersburg. It was much visited by nobility travelling from the old capital to the new one and back.

Following a great fire of 1763, the city was rebuilt in Neoclassical style. Under Catherine the Great, the downtown was thoroughly reconstructed. Crumbling medieval edifices were razed and replaced with imposing Neoclassical structures. The most important of these are the Travel Palace of the Empress (designed by the celebrated Matvei Kazakov), and the Ascension church (designed by Prince Lvov and consecrated in 1813).

In 1931, the city was renamed Kalinin, after a notable Soviet leader Mikhail Kalinin who had been born nearby. A last vestige of the pre-Petrine epoch, the Saviour Cathedral, was blown up in 1936. In 1940 the NKVD executed more than 6,200 Polish policemen and prisoners of war from Ostashkov camp. During the Cold War it was home to the air base Kryuchkovo, which is no longer in service. The historic name of Tver was restored in 1990.

The Wehrmacht occupied Kalinin for two months in 1941, leaving the city in ashes. A large-scale resistance movement in the city and the region resulted in over 30,000 Nazi soldiers and officers eliminated during the occupation of the city. Kalinin was the first major city in Europe to be liberated from the Wehrmacht.
