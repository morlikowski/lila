The 47 species of '''sac-winged''' or '''sheathtailed bats''' constitute the family '''Emballonuridae''', and can be found in tropical and sub-tropical regions all over the world. Emballonurids include some of the smallest of all bats, and range from 3.5 to 10 cm in body length. They are generally brown or grey, although the ghost bats (genus ''Diclidurus'') are white.

They have short tails, which project through the tail membrane so that the latter forms a sheath. As their name indicates, most species also possess sac-shaped glands in their wings, which are open to the air and may release dental formula: 

These bats prefer to roost in areas generally lighter than other species of bats. Their dwellings can often be found in hollow trees and entryways to caves or other structures. Some, such as the tomb bats live in large roosts, but others are solitary. Species living away from the tropics may become torpid, or even hibernate in winter<ref name=EoM/>.
