The '''Blackwater River''' is a  long Black Fork, it is a principal Monongahela and the Ohio Rivers, it is part of the watershed of the blackwater stream, owing to spruce and hemlock trees in its watershed, the tannins of which impart a tea or amber color to its water.<ref name=WVenc/>  

==Geography==
The entire course and drainage area of the Blackwater lie within Tucker County.<ref name=DeLorme>DeLorme (1997).  ''West Virginia Atlas & Gazetteer''.  Yarmouth, Maine: DeLorme.  ISBN 0-89933-246-3.</ref>
It rises at an elevation of 3,250 ft (991 m)<ref name=WVenc/> and initially meanders north-northeastwardly through the Canaan Valley, passing through the Davis, it falls  over whitewater rapids of Class IV-V+<ref>[http://www.americanwhitewater.org/rivers/id/2334 American Whitewater report on lower Blackwater River]</ref> and collects the eight-mile long '''North Fork Blackwater River''' <ref></ref>, which flows past the town of Thomas. Finally, the Blackwater joins the Dry Fork to form the Black Fork at the town of Hendricks,<ref name=DeLorme/> at an elevation of .<ref name="GNIS Blackwater River"/>

==Water quality==
===Blackwater===
As with other rhododendron, mountain laurel and the sphagnum bogs of Canaan Valley also contribute. The Blackwater is also typical in having transparent, acidic, and oligotrophic (very low nutrient) water.
 
===Acid mine drainage===
Beginning in the 1880s, the Blackwater River watershed (especially the segment of the North Fork of the Blackwater River between the towns of Thomas and Douglas) suffered extreme environmental degradation due to deep mining and coke production. This was exacerbated by the surface mining activities of the 1960s and '70s, which brought about a severe decline in water quality due to acid mine drainage (AMD). 

In recent years, the strata are exposed to air and rainwater, such as when surface mining occurs. The sulfuric acid produced from the sulfite reaction can dissolve metals such as iron, aluminum, and magnesium.<ref>[http://mine-drainage.usgs.gov/pubs/ICARD_2000_Sibrell.pdf Sibrell, P.L.,  Barnaby J. Watten, B.J., Friedrich, A.E, and Vinci, B.J., ''ARD Remediation with Limestone in a CO2 Pressurized Reactor'', 5th International Conference on Acid Rock Drainage, Denver, Colorado, May, 2000.]</ref>  By 2004, the combination of acid and dissolved metals drainage had destroyed or damaged about 12,000 miles (19,300 km) of streams in Appalachia,<ref>[http://www.flyrodreel.com/index.php/page/issues/sku/FRR2004_0710/id/99 Williams, T., "Coal Country Trout", ''Fly Rod & Reel'' Magazine, July/October 2004.]</ref> including the Blackwater River and streams in its watershed. 

Restoration of the Blackwater River watershed was a costly<ref name=AmdTF> [http://wvmdtaskforce.com/proceedings/99/99SKO/99SKO.HTM Skousen, J., Gorman, J., and Ziemkiewicz, P., ''Long Term Effects of Acid Mine Drainage Remediation Projects on Stream Quality'', Proceedings Twentieth Annual West Virginia Surface Mine Drainage Task Force Symposium, Morgantown, WV, April 13-14, 1999.]</ref><ref name=AmdTF99>[http://wvmdtaskforce.com/proceedings/96/96ZUR/96ZUR.HTM Zurbuch, P.E., ''Early Results from Calcium Carbonate Neutralization of Two West Virginia Rivers Acidified by Mine Drainage'', Seventeenth Annual West Virginia Surface Mine Drainage Task Force Symposium, Morgantown, WV, April 2-3, 1996.] </ref> and complex task involving two large projects.  The Douglas Highwall Abandoned Mine Land Reclamation Project installed 16,000 tons (14,400 tonnes) of limestone in a "Wetland/Anoxic Limestone Drain (WALD) system"<ref name=AmdTF/> on the North Fork of the Blackwater downstream of Thomas.  Although the experimental WALD system did not perform as intended, and "has not generated measurable alkalinity"<ref name=AmdTF/> it did reduce the total acid and metal load in the water discharge from the abandoned mine into the river system.

More successful was a project just upstream of Davis, on Beaver Creek, a tributary of the Blackwater.<ref>[http://www.mii.org/blackwater/blackwater.html Mineral Information Institute web site, accessed August 26, 2006.]</ref> A six-drum water-powered limestone grinding station adds limestone slurry to the water flowing down Beaver Creek. A Swedish-designed limestone powder dosing system was also installed as backup to the drum system.<ref name=AmdTF99/>  The five mile stretch of the Blackwater between the limestone station and the confluence with the North Fork of the Blackwater has now become one of West Virginia's premiere catch and release trout streams.<ref>[http://www.wvdnr.gov/Fishing/SAFRegs.shtm West Virginia Department of Natural Resources Special Fishing Regulations web page, accessed August 27, 2006.]</ref>

Der '''Blackwater River''' ist ein 50&nbsp;km langer Vereinigten Staaten. Über den Black Fork ist er ein indirekter Einzugsgebietes des Mississippi Rivers und entwässert ein Gebiet von 368&nbsp;km². 

== Geographie ==

Der Blackwater River ist ein Schwarzwasserfluss, was ihm auch den Namen gab. Der gesamte Verlauf des Flusses liegt innerhalb des Tucker Countys. Er entspringt in 991&nbsp;m Höhe<ref name=WVenc/> und durchquert zunächst durch die Feuchtgebiete des Canaan Valleys im Canaan Valley Resort State Park und im Canaan Valley National Wildlife Refuge. Danach nimmt er die beiden Zuflüsse ''North Branch Blackwater River''<ref></ref> und ''Little Blackwater River''<ref></ref> auf. Danach wendet er sich für den Rest seines Laufes nach Südwesten.<ref name=DeLorme>DeLorme (Hrsg.): ''West Virginia Atlas & Gazetteer.'' DeLorme, Yarmouth 1997, ISBN 0-89933-246-3.</ref> 

Nachdem der Fluss an der Stadt Davis vorbeigeflossen ist, stürzt er über die Hendricks fließt er mit dem Dry Fork zusammen und bildet den Black Fork. Die Mündung liegt in einer Höhe von 520&nbsp;m.<ref name=WVenc/>
