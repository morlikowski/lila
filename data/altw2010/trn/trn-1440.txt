'''Pluak Daeng''' () is a district (''Amphoe'') in the northwestern part of Rayong Province, eastern Thailand.

==Etymology==
The name Pluak Daeng originates from the founding of the village. The villages found an Ant hill full with the Red Termites (Thai: ''Pluak Daeng''), different from those in other areas.

==History==
The area was originally a part of Ban Khai district. The minor district (''King Amphoe'') Pluak Daeng was established on June 1 1970 consisting of the two ''tambon'' Ta Sit and Pluak Daeng.<ref></ref> It was upgraded to a full district on March 25 1979.<ref></ref>

==Geography==
Neighboring districts are (from the east clockwise) Wang Chan, Ban Khai, Nikhom Phatthana of Rayong Province, Bang Lamung, Si Racha and Nong Yai of Chonburi Province.
