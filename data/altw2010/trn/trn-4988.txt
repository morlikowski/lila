'''Teppo Numminen''' est un défenseur de finlandais. Né à Tappara. En 127 matchs avec ce club, il récolta 55 points.

Il fut par la suite repêché par les Jets de Winnipeg au repêchage d'entrée dans la LNH 1986 au 29 rang (choix de 2 ronde). Il jouera par la suite 15 saisons avec cette organisation, vivant le démenagement de l'équipe de Winnipeg à Phoenix où le club deviendra les Coyotes de Phoenix. Son association avec Winnipeg/Phoenix prit fin le 22 juillet 2003 quand les Coyotes l'ont transfé aux Stars de Dallas en retour de Mike Sillinger, il n'y jouera qu'une saison avant de signer un contrat avec les Sabres de Buffalo où il y joue toujours.

== Exploits ==
Il a remporté 2 médailles au Jeux Olympiques : une première fois lorsqu'il met la main sur la médaille d'argent en 1988 et une nouvelle fois, toujours l'argent, en 2006. 
Il participa a 3 matchs des étoiles (1999, 2000 et 2001).
Il est devenu le 13 novembre 2006 l'Européen ayant joué le plus grand nombre de matchs dans la LNH, en jouant ce jour là son 1252 match. Il a surpassé Jari Kurri autre idole du hockey finlandais.

'''Teppo Numminen''' (born hockey defenceman. He is currently a Unrestricted free agent.
==Playing career==
Numminen started his career with his local team, 1996–97 season. He played in the NHL All-Star Game in both 1999 and 2000. After fifteen seasons playing for the Winnipeg/Phoenix organization, Numminen was traded to the Dallas Stars in July, 2003, for Mike Sillinger. Numminen took a break from hockey during the 2004–05 NHL lockout. Numminen then signed as a free agent with the Buffalo Sabres. 

On November 13, 2006, Numminen played his 1252<sup>nd</sup> game in the NHL and in the process, set a record for most NHL games played as a European-trained player, surpassing Jari Kurri. Numminen is the only player from the 1986 NHL Entry Draft who is still playing in the NHL.

After signing a one-year contract for the 2007–08 NHL season, Numminen was forced to undergo open heart surgery before the start of the season. Numminen was suspended by the Buffalo Sabres when it was discovered he needed heart surgery.<ref>http://www.buffalonews.com/sports/sabresnhl/story/162294.html</ref>. Buffalo Sabres GM Darcy Regier stated the suspension was due to the fact that his $2.6 million dollar contract was not insured,and there's a clause explicitly in the contract that states the player must pass the physical and be fit to play.<ref>http://www.buffalonews.com/sports/story/162818.html</ref>

Word came that Numminen's heart surgery was successful.  Completed on 2007–08 season on April 5, 2008.

As per Buffalo Sabres General Manager- Darcy Regier, Numminen has expressed some interest in returning and continuing NHL play for at least another year.

===International play===
Numminen has won two silver medals ('88 & '06) and a bronze ('98) playing with the Finnish Olympic Team.

== Other activities==
Teppo Numminen owns 90 % of [http://www.montrealhockey.com/index/en/home/company/info.html Montreal Sports Ltd,] which is a company founded by Teppo's father Kalevi "Kallu" Numminen. The company produces ice hockey sticks and equipment.

==Personal life==
Teppo and his wife Ann-Maarit have two daughters, Bianca and Erica, and one son, Niklas.
