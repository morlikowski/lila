'''Sistem planet''' terdiri daripada pelbagai objek bukan najam yang beredar mengelilingi bulan, asteroid, meteor, komet, dan habuk kosmik. Kesemuanya, satu atau lebih bintang dan sistem planetnya membentuk sistem bintang. Sistem planet kita sendiri, yang mengandungi Bumi, bersama Matahari membentuk sistem bintang yang unik iaitu sistem suria.

Sistem planet mengelilingi bintang seperti matahari pada umumnya dipercayai terbentuk melalui proses yang sama yang menghasilkan pembentukan bintang. Sesetengah teori awal membabitkan bintang lain melalui hampir dengan matahari, dan menarik keluar jisim darinya yang kemudiannya bergumpal dan membentuk planet. Bagaimanapun, kemungkinan peristiwa hampir berlanggar kini diketahui terlalu kecil untuk menjayakan model ini. Teori yang diterima masa kini menegaskan bahawa sistem planet terbentuk dari nebula suria.

Sesetengah sistem planet tidak sama dengan sistem suria. Walau bagaimanapun, sistem planet sekeliling pulsar telah disimpulkan dari variasi seni dalam tempoh denyutan radiasi eletromagnetik. Pulsar terbentuk dalam letupan ganas supernova, dan sistem planet normal tidak mungkin berjaya selamat letupan sebegitu - planet kemungkinannya akan lenyap, atau kehilangan mengejut kebanyakan jisim bintang pusat akan menjadikan ia terbebas daripada tarikan graviti bintang tersebut. Satu teori menyatakan bahawa teman stellar sedia ada hampir lenyap sepenuhnya akibat letupan supernova, meninggalkan badan bersaiz planet. Selain itu, planet mungkin terbentuk melalui cakera akresi yang mengelilingi pulsar.

== Origin and Evolution ==
right|thumb|250px|An artist's concept of a [[protoplanetary disk]]
Planetary systems are generally believed to form as part of the same process which results in star formation.  Some early theories involved another star passing extremely close to the Sun, drawing material out from it which then coalesced to form the planets.  However, the probability of such a near collision is now known to be far too low to make this a viable model.  Accepted theories today argue that a protoplanetary disk forms by gravitational collapse of a molecular cloud and then evolves into  a planetary system by collisions and gravitational capture.<ref>[http://www.daviddarling.info/encyclopedia/P/plansysform.html planetary systems, formation of], David Darling, entry in ''The Internet Encyclopedia of Science'', accessed on line September 23, 2007.</ref>

Some planetary systems may form differently, however.  Planets orbiting pulsars&mdash;stars which  emit periodic bursts of electromagnetic radiation&mdash;have been discovered by the slight variations they cause in the timing of these bursts.  Pulsars are formed in violent supernova explosions, and a normal planetary system could not possibly survive such a blast&mdash;planets would either evaporate, or the sudden loss of most of the mass of the central star would see them escape the gravitational hold of the star.  One theory is that existing stellar companions were almost entirely evaporated by the supernova blast, leaving behind planet-sized bodies.  Alternatively, planets may somehow form in the accretion disk surrounding pulsars.<ref>[http://adsabs.harvard.edu/abs/1993ASPC...36..149P Planet formation scenarios], Philipp Podsiadlowski, pp. 149&ndash;165, in ''Planets around pulsars; Proceedings of the Conference, California Inst. of Technology, Pasadena, Apr. 30-May 1, 1992'', edited by J. A. Phillips, J. E. Thorsest, and S. R. Kulkarni, ASP Conference Series, '''36''', 1993.</ref>
