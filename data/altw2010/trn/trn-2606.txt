==History==
As the "English and Dutch, who were repelled by the Portuguese. The Treaty of Utrecht, in 1713 established the boundaries between Brazil and French Guiana, but these were not respected by the French. In the 18th century, France retook control of the area. This dispute would continue until 1900.

With the discovery of river of the same name). It became the federal territory of Amapá in 1943. 

The discovery of rich manganese deposits in Serra do Navio in 1945 revolutionized the local economy, but Amapá did not achieve statehood until October 5th, 1988 with the promulgation of the new Brazilian Constitution.

==Geography==
thumb|right|[[Amazon Rainforest.]]
The State of Amapá has also the lowest deforestation rate of its original vegetation: only 2%. Most of its territory is occupied by forests, with the remaining area corresponding to savannah and plain areas. At the seaside, almost intact beaches get mixed with swamps, composing the largest Brazilian representation of this biome, where this mix of salt and fresh water is perfect for the reproduction of the food chain for several animal species.

===Vegetation===
The Amazon represents over half of the planet's remaining rainforests and comprises the largest and most species-rich tract of tropical rainforest in the world. Wet tropical forests are the most species-rich biome, and tropical forests in the Americas are consistently more species rich than the wet forests in Africa and Asia.<ref name ="Turner 2001">Turner, I.M. 2001. ''The ecology of trees in the tropical rain forest''.  Cambridge University Press, Cambridge.  ISBN 0-521-80183-4</ref>  As the largest tract of tropical rainforest in the Americas, the Amazonian rainforests have unparalleled biodiversity. ==Demographics==
According to the IBGE of 2007, there were 619,000 people residing in the state with a population density of 4.3/km².

Urbanization: 93.7% (2006); Population growth: 5.7% (1991-2000); Houses: 144,000 (2006).<ref>Source: PNAD.</ref>

The last PNAD (National Research for Sample of Domiciles) census revealed the following numbers: 429,000 Brown (mixed) people (69.4%), 148,000 White people (24.0%), 40,000 Black people (6.5%), 6,000 Asian or Amerindian people (0.1%).<ref></ref>

==Economy==
The service sector is the largest component of GDP at 87.6%, followed by the industrial sector at 7.8%. Agriculture represents 4.6% of GDP (2004). Amapá exports: wood 75.5%, ores 18.7%, heart of palm 5.5% (2002). 

Share of the Brazilian economy: 0.2% (2005).

'''アマパー州'''（''Estado de Amapá''）はブラジルの北部に位置する州、州庁所在地はマカパ。略称は「'''AP'''」である。

== 位置 ==
大西洋に注ぐアマゾン川の河口の北側に位置しており、大西洋に面したブラジルの州では最北である。北にフランス領ギアナとスリナム、その他をパラー州と面している。

アマゾンの一角として自然が多く残されており、国立公園も設置されている。
この州は全域がブラジリア時間と同じである。
