'''François Marie ''Louis'' de Maigret''', Count de Maigret & de Neau, Baron de Stockem, Count of the Holy Roman Empire (December 2, 1914 - June 13, 2001), was a French military officer of World War II.

==Life and career==
Born at his family estate Saint-Romain-sous-Versigny, he entered the officer school Saint-Cyr in 1935, and graduated with honours in 1937, promotion Maréchal Lyautey.

Believing in the colonial Empire's importance to France, de Maigret decided to follow Leclerc and joined the "Armée d'Afrique". At this time he refounded the Goumiers with General Guillaume. They then fought in Tunisia (1942-43), Italy, (1943-45), mainland France (1944) and Germany (1944-45), where they liberated Pforzheim and Stuttgart.

He was next sent to command the French forces in Germany after WWII, and fought again in Indochina, till 1954, when he was named a Lieutenant-Colonel and moved to Italy.

He then fought in de Gaulle: "This is not any more the France I want to serve."

He settled down in France on his family estates, for many years being one of the most decorated colonels of the French army.

He kept an excellent reputation and was known for keeping a wolf as a pet. He was also famous for his international friendship with "history makers", such as the last Emperor of Viet-Nam, Bao Dai, and members of the European royal families.

==Family==
The Comte Louis de Maigret was son of Christian, Count de Maigret, one of the main heirs of the Moët & Chandon dynasty, and of Marie d'Anterroches, herself descendent of the famous Count d'Anterroches, one of the most important general in the Battle of Fontenoy against England.

==Biographie==
Porté vers le métier des armes, il entre à l'école spéciale militaire de Saint-Cyr en 1935, pour en sortir en bonne position en 1937, promotion Maréchal Lyautey.

Sa carrière militaire le conduit à rejoindre l'Armée d'Afrique, il décide de suivre le Général de Lattre et sert sous le commandement du Général Guillaume avec lequel il forme les goumiers. Il débarque en Italie, combat à la Bataille du mont Cassin et participe aux campagnes de lutte contre les armées allemandes dans les Vosges et en Alsace. Il franchit le Rhin en mars 1945 et participe à la prise de Pforzheim puis de Stuttgart.

Des quatre frères du comte Louis de Maigret, trois mourront pour la France entre 1940 et 1944.

Il est ensuite en poste au commandement des Forces Françaises en Allemagne, puis est envoyé en Italie. Sa carrière le mène ensuite à participer activement aux Guerres d'Indochine puis d'Algérie en tant que lieutenant-colonel.
