'''ウィントン・ルーファー'''（Wynton Alan Whai Rufer、サッカー選手（FW）。

== 来歴 ==
ニュージーランドのクラブチームで経験を積んだ後、1シーズンあまりをイングランドで過ごし、スイスのクラブチーム・チューリッヒへ移籍。その後スイスのクラブチームを転々とし、グラスホッパーではタイトル獲得に貢献。そこでの活躍が認められ、ドイツ・ブンデスリーガ・ヴェルダー・ブレーメンへ移籍。1989年、1990年、1992年と3度オセアニア年間最優秀選手に輝くなど選手としての本領を発揮するとともに、1992-1993シーズンにはリーグ優勝を果たすなどここでもタイトル獲得に活躍。翌1993-1994シーズンにはブンデスリーガの最優秀外国人選手に選出された。

Jリーグ・ジェフユナイテッド市原に移籍。25ゴールを挙げる活躍を見せると2年間在籍、カイザースラウテルンを経て母国のクラブチームに移籍し、1997年に一時引退。1999年にはニュージーランド初のプロクラブ・フットボールキングズのプレイヤーコーチとして現場復帰した。

現在は1997年に立ち上げたサッカースクール'''ウィナーズ'''（WYNRS）で後進の指導にあたっている。

'''Wynton Alan Whai "Kiwi" Rufer''' CNZM (born Swiss father and a mother of Northern Thunder F.C.. He was a New Zealand international footballer remembered as his country's greatest ever player, finishing his international career with 38 caps and 17 goals. His Brother Shane Rufer works currently for YoungHeart Manawatu as Head Coach.

==Playing Career==
After graduating from Rongotai College, Rufer began his playing career with Norwich City manager Ken Brown, who invited Wynton and his brother Shane Rufer to Norfolk for a trial.

Rufer impressed and signed a professional contract on FC Zurich in May 1982, having helped New Zealand to the 1982 FIFA World Cup, scoring the winning goal in a qualification play-off against China.<ref name="rufer herald">[http://www.nzherald.co.nz/event/story.cfm?c_id=523&ObjectID=283515 Rufer - simply the best we've had]</ref>

During his time in FC Grasshoppers, winning the Swiss Cup in 1988-1989 before moving to Werder Bremen, managed by Otto Rehhagel.

Rufer was an enormous success as part of Rehhagel's team, winning the Bundesliga success, and he was the top scorer in the 1993-1994 UEFA Champions League tournament, winning another German Cup that same season<ref name="rufer herald" />.

Rufer was also voted Oceania's Player of the Year in 1989, 1990 and 1992.<ref name="fifa wynr" /><ref>[http://www.rsssf.com/miscellaneous/oce-poy.html Oceania Player of the Year]</ref>

During the 1994-1995 season, Rufer left Bremen to join German top flight - Rufer's contribution helped the club win the Bundesliga II, although he left before Rehhagel remarkably won the Bundesliga the next season. 

In 1997 he returned to his homeland, joining Central United and establishing his own coaching school, Wynrs, producing Kiwi football stars such as Mario Hofmann and Michael Fitzgerald, as well as women's international Annalie Longo<ref name="fifa wynr" />.

Along with his brother Shane, Rufer took on player-coaching duties at FC Kingz (later renamed Auckland Kingz), participating in the Australian Soccer League for two seasons before finally retiring in 2001, having been named Oceania's Player of the Century ahead of Frank Farina (Australia) and Christian Karembeu (New Caledonia/France)<ref name="rufer herald" />.
