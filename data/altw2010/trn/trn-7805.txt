'''James Robert''' "'''Jim'''" '''Davis''' (born American Tumbleweeds'', ''Gnorm Gnat'', ''U.S. Acres'' a.k.a. Orson's Farm, and a strip about Mr. Potato Head.  He has written Emmy-award winning TV specials and was also one of the producers behind the "Garfield & Friends" TV show which aired on CBS from 1988-1995. In addition, he is currently the writer and co-producer of a trilogy of CGI-direct-to-video feature films about Garfield; the first one, "Garfield Gets Real", was released in 2007.

==Biography==
===Personal===
Jim Davis was born in Marion, where he grew up on a small farm with his father James William Davis, mother Anna Catherine (Carter) Davis, brother Dave, and 25 Jon Arbuckle, who was also raised on a farm with his parents and a brother, Doc Boy. Jon, too, is a cartoonist, and also celebrates his birthday on July 28.
Davis attended fraternity. He earned the dubious honor of earning one of the lowest cumulative grade point averages in the history of the university, an honor incidentally shared with ''Late Show'' host David Letterman.

Ironically, considering his fame as a cartoonist who draws a cat, his first wife Carolyn (Altekruse) was allergic to cats<ref name="Time">"Those Catty Cartoonists,"  ''Time'' magazine, Dec. 07, 1981; available online at [http://www.time.com/time/magazine/article/0,9171,953256-1,00.html Time magazine] website.
</ref> although they owned a dog named Molly<ref name="everything2">[http://everything2.com/index.pl?node=Jim%20Davis Jim Davis at Everything2.com]
</ref>.  They have a son, James Alexander Davis<ref>[http://www.nndb.com/people/523/000023454/ NNDB profile], accessed March 15 2008</ref><ref name="Time"/>  On July 16, 2000, Davis married his current wife Jill. They have three children: James, Ashley, and Christopher.<ref name="everything2"/>

Davis as of 2007 resides in Albany, Indiana, where he and his staff produce ''Garfield'' under his company, Paws, Inc., begun in 1981. Paws, Inc. employs nearly 50 artists and licensing administrators, who work with agents around the world managing Garfield's vast licensing, syndication, and entertainment empire.

Davis and his wife Jill, Sr. VP of Licensing at Paws, have three children and three grandchildren.

===Professional===
Prior to creating Garfield, Davis worked for a local advertising agency and in 1969 began assisting Tom Ryan's comic strip, ''Tumbleweeds''. He then created a comic strip, ''Gnorm Gnat'', that ran for five years in ''The Pendleton Times'', an Indiana newspaper. Davis tried to sell it to a national comic strip syndicate, but an editor told him, "Your art is good, your gags are great, but bugs &mdash; nobody can relate with bugs!"

On June 19, 1978, ''Garfield'' started syndication in 41 newspapers. Things were going well until the Chicago Sun-Times cancelled the strip, prompting an outcry from 1300 readers. Garfield was reinstated and the strip quickly became the fastest selling comic strip in the world.  Today it is syndicated in 2400 newspapers and is read by approximately 200 million readers each day. 

In the 1988-1994 cartoon series Garfield and Friends, one episode has a scene where Garfield slid down a fireman's pole in a haunted house, and Davis has a brief cameo as himself drawing a cartoon.
