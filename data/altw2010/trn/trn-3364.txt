'''Карл III ''Дитя''''' (, ок. короля Франции Карла II Лысого и Ирментруды Орлеанской.

== Биография ==
Карл был вторым сыном короля Франции Карла II Лысого, младшим братом Людовика Заики. В октябре Акитании, опекуном которого стал граф Пуатье и герцог Аквитании Рамнульф I. Карл Дитя был коронован в Лиможе. Но в отличие от предыдущих королей реальной власти Карл Дитя не имел. Аквитания находилась в подчинении короля Франции. В королевстве отсутствовала канцелярия, все назначения производил Карл Лысый. Реальное управление королевством находилось в руках совета, который возглавлял герцог Рамнульф I. 

В Компьене. Жена Карла Дитя была удалена от двора. Но к 865 году Карл Лысый снова снова вернул сына в Аквитанию. 

В 864 году Карл Дитя случайно получил на охоте сильный удар мечом по голове, после чего страдал эпилепсией<ref>Lexikon des Mittelalters: Band V Spalte 969; [http://www.mittelalter-genealogie.de/karolinger_westfraenkische_linie/karl_das_kind_frankenkoenig_866.html genealogie-mittelalter.de mit dem Artikel aus dem Lexikon des Mittelalters]</ref>. Он умер бездетным в 866 году. Похоронили его в Бурже в церкви Св. Сульпиция.

== Брак ==
Жена: с 862 '''Ансгарда''' (?), вдова Гумберта, графа Буржа

'''Charles III of Aquitaine<ref>Charles I being Charlemagne, who ruled Aquitaine as part of his inheritance and then when he succeeded fully in the Kingdom of the Franks; Charles II being Charles the Bald. </ref>''', called '''Charles the Child''' (in the Latin of the ''Annales Bertiniani'', ''Karolus puer'') (c. 847-848, Frankfurt am Main &ndash; 29 September 866, Buzançais, Indre ''département'') was the King of Aquitaine from October 855 until his death in 866.

He was the second son of Pippin II. Pippin succeeded in rallying the nobles to support himself and Charles the Bald against Louis the Younger, who was driven out. By October, however, Pippin lost his popularity with the still-rebellious Aquitainians, prompting Charles the Bald to appoint Charles the Child as King. Charles the Child was duly anointed at Limoges. Within a year, he had been replaced by the Aquitainians with Pippin II; these same then deposed Pippin and restored Charles the Child. Pippin was then captured in 864 and imprisoned at Senlis, from whence he disappears from history.

Unlike previous sub-kings of Aquitaine, (Pippin I, Pippin II), Charles the Child had no real authority at all. Before 840, the Kingdom had been ruled in person by an autonomous king; Charles the Bald, however, after his accession as King of Western Francia, attempted to maintain power in Aquitaine. Consequently, Charles the Child, and his brother, Louis the Stammerer, did not rule in person, had no chancery, could issue no instruments; they were no longer empowered to bestow privileges, endow religious establishments, or dispose of royal property. All the rights of the region were invested in Charles the Bald, in whose absence the nobles of the Kingdom gathered power.  

Nonetheless, as Charles grew older, he began to exercise what little personal authority he could: for example, he chose and married   a wife in 862 against the will of his father. His wife is unknown, although she was apparently the widow of a count named Humbert. Charles the Bald however reasserted his power over his son in 863, forcing the younger Charles to put away his wife and be loyal to his father. A year later, he was accidentally struck in the head by a sword-swipe; the blow left him mentally incapacitated until his resultant death in 866. He died childless, and was buried in Bourges.<ref>Callahan, 34.</ref>
