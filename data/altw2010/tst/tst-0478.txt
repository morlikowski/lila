==Events of 1827==
===January - June===
    <!-- 
    Link all dates, even if repeated, for date-format preferences.
    -->
* South America: Battle of Ituzaingo or Passo do Rosário - A force of the Brazilian Imperial Army meets Argentinean-Uruguayan troops in combat.
* America offering commercial transportation of both people and freight.
* Rio Negro and attack the temporary naval base of Carmen de Patagones, Argentina. They are defeated by the local citizens.
* abducted by Edward Gibbon Wakefield, the future politician in colonial New Zealand.
* March 16 - ''Freedom's Journal'', the first African-American owned and published newspaper in the United States, is founded in New York City by John Russwurm.
* Ottoman Algeria: Husain Dei slaps the French consul Decalina on the face, eventually leading to war and French rule in Algeria.
* 8 - Battle of Monte Santiago: Naval engagement between a squadron of the Brazilian Imperial Navy and Argentinean vessels. Major Brazilian victory.
* UK: Lord Liverpool as British Prime Minister.
* April 24 - Greek War of Independence: The Battle of Phaleron is fought. 
* Baltimore and hosts its first meeting at the Baltimore Atheneum.

===July - December===
300px|thumb|right|[[October 20:''Naval Battle of Navarino'' by Carneray]]
* Treaty of London between Britain, and Turks agree to an armistice in Greece.
* Diocese of Honolulu founded. 
* UK: UK, following the death of George Canning.
* September 4 - Finland: Great Fire of Turku.
* September 22 - Joseph Smith Jr. obtains the Book of Mormon, for him to translate.
* October 1 - Russo-Persian War, 1826-1828: The Russians under Ivan Paskevich storm Yerevan, ending a millennium of Muslim domination in Eastern Armenia.
* British, French, and Turko-Egyptian fleet in Greece.

===Undated===
* Laos: Siam and successfully attacks Siamese later invade Vientiane and nearly destroy the whole city). 
* John Walker invents the first friction match which he names ''Lucifer''. 
* Egypt: Cairo University School of Medicine is established as the first African medical school in the Middle East.
