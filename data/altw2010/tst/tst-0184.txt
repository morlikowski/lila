'''Веттер''' () — город в Германии, в федеральной земле административному округу Гиссен. Входит в состав района Марбург-Биденкопф.  Население составляет 9311 человек (на 31 декабря 2006 года). Занимает площадь 104,56 км². Официальный код 06 5 34 021.

Город подразделяется на 9 городских районов.

'''Wetter''' is a small town in Hesse, Hessen-Nassau)''' stems from a time when the town belonged to the Prussian province of the same name, and nowadays is only used by the railway – even today, the railway station in town bears this name.
thumb|none|View of the Monastery Church

== Geography ==
Wetter lies in the Marburg-Biedenkopf district on the western edge of the ''Burgwald'', a low mountain range, in the Wetschaft valley and neighbouring places about 14&nbsp;km north of Marburg.

=== Neighbouring communities ===
In the north, Wetter borders on the town of Rosenthal in Rauschenberg and the community of Cölbe, in the south on the community of Lahntal, in the southwest on the community of Dautphetal, and in the southwest on the town of Biedenkopf and the community of Münchhausen am Christenberg, all in Marburg-Biedenkopf district.

== Transport ==
North to south through the town's municipal area runs the Federal Highway (''Bundesstraße'') B 252 from eastern Frankenberg and on to Göttingen (in the community of bypass road is planned.

The ''Burgwaldbahn'' railway line connects the town with Marburg and Frankenberg. The ''Kurhessenbahn'' which runs the line means to reopen the extension between Frankenberg and Korbach.

== History ==
Wetter was already being mentioned in documents in the 8th and 9th centuries; it was mentioned in one such document under the same name that it still has today in 1108, and the "''Weistum'' of Wetter" was already making its town rights plain to see even in 1239.

=== Coat of arms ===
Wetter's civic coat of arms might be described thus: In Or on a three-knolled hill vert a fleur-de-lis twig vert with three blossoms argent flanked by two inescutcheons, dexter in azure the Hessian Lion rampant gules and argent sinister; sinister in gules the wheel of Mainz argent.<!---Badly butchered I'm sure. Feel free to make corrections.--->

The two inescutcheons (smaller shields within the main one) hark back to the time when the Hessian Landgraves held sway (the Hessian Lion), and to the Archbishops of Mainz (the wheel of Mainz).
