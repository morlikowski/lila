'''Gene Barry'''  (born '''Eugene Klass'''; American actor.

==Biography==
===Career===
Barry adopted his professional name in honor of Broadway in ''Catherine Was Great'' in 1944, and returned many years later to star in the 1983 musical ''La Cage aux Folles'' (based on the French film of the same name about a gay couple with a straight son) with George Hearn as his life partner/spouse. For his contribution to live theater, Gene Barry has a star on the Hollywood Walk of Fame at 6555 Hollywood Blvd.

A prominent early The War of the Worlds''. He made an eyeblink-length War of the Worlds'', along with his 1953 co-star Ann Robinson.

Known for his suave manner, Barry was featured on Bat Masterson'', ''The Name of the Game'', and ''Golden Globe for ''Burke's Law''. The series, featuring homicide investigations by a millionaire police captain, returned in 1993-94 with Barry once again in the title role. 

He also starred in the 1973 The Adventurer'', with Guns of Paradise'' in 1990 alongside Wyatt Earp and again the following year in ''The Gambler Returns: Luck of the Draw'', also with O'Brian as Earp.

Barry portrayed the murderer in the original two-hour pilot for the television mystery series ''Columbo'', a psychiatrist who kills his wife in ''Prescription: Murder''.
