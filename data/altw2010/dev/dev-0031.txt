'''Finland''' competed at the '''2006 Winter Olympics''' in Turin, Italy, with 102 athletes competing in 11 of the 15 sports.
<ref> [http://www.torinokisasivut.fi/torino_2006_olympiakisat/suomen_joukkue/joukkue_lajeittain/ Torino-Kisavisut - Joukkue lajeittain], from Suomen Olympiakomitea (Finnish Olympic Committee), retrieved 26 January 2006</ref>

Janne Lahtela, a moguls freestyle skier, was the flag bearer at the Opening Ceremonies.

===Biathlon===

Paavo Puurunen is the only Finnish biathlete who is set to travel to Torino<ref> [http://www.torinokisasivut.fi/torino_2006_olympiakisat/suomen_joukkue/joukkue_lajeittain/ampumahiihto/ Torino-Kisasivut - Ampumahiihto], from SOK, retrieved 26 January 2006</ref>. He is placed 62nd in the overall biathlon World Cup<ref>[http://data.biathlonworld.com/data/archives.aspx?CupId=BT0506SWRLCP__SMTS World Cup Total Score Men - Standings after 14 of 26 competitions], from biathlonworld.com, retrieved 26 January 2006</ref>

===Curling===

Finland are sending a men's curling team to the Olympics<ref> [http://www.torinokisasivut.fi/torino_2006_olympiakisat/suomen_joukkue/joukkue_lajeittain/curling/ Torino-Kisasivut - Curling], from SOK, retrieved 26 January 2006</ref>, the same team which finished fifth at the 2005 Ford World Men's Curling Championship. The positions in brackets are those which the players had at the 2005 Worlds.

* Markku Uusipaavalniemi (skip)
* Wille Mäkelä (third)
* Kalle Kiiskinen (second)
* Teemu Salo (lead)
* Jani Sullanmaa (did not play at the 2005 Worlds, presumably reserve)
