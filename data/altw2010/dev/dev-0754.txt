'''Jonathan Charles Palmer''' (Londra, Regno Unito,  7 novembre 1956) è un ex-pilota di Formula 1. 

Prima di partecipare ai campionati di Formula 1, nel 1983 ha ottenuto la vittoria finale nel campionato europeo di Formula 2 al volante di una Ralt-Honda, che gli è valsa la chiamata di Frank Williams per debuttare in Formula 1 al Gp d'Europa della stessa stagione.

Vincitore del ''Trofeo Jim Clark'' come miglior pilota in una vettura senza stagione 1987, quando colse al Gran Premio d'Australia un 4&deg; posto con la Tyrrell, miglior risultato in carriera.

Dr. '''Jonathan Charles Palmer''' (born turbocharged engines, with team-mate Philippe Streiff his main rival. He also had a spell as McLaren test driver in 1990.

Before opting for a career in motor racing, Palmer qualified and worked as a doctor in London.

After retiring from driving Palmer was drafted in as a commentator by the BBC in mid-1993 to replace heart attack shortly after the Canadian Grand Prix. Although his commentary alongside veteran Murray Walker was knowledgeable and articulate, when coverage moved to ITV in 1997 Palmer was replaced by Martin Brundle.

Palmer was impersonated by Jonathan Kern, an international conman who used his name all over Europe and the USA.<ref>. Sunday Times  (Johannesburg), January 12, 2003. Accessed 18 April 2007.</ref>

Palmer is the name behind the Formula Palmer Audi open wheel racing series in the UK, and has managed the career of its inaugural champion Justin Wilson for some time.

MotorSport Vision, a company headed by Palmer, owns a number of motor racing circuits in the UK. These include Snetterton, Cadwell Park, Oulton Park and Bedford Autodrome.
