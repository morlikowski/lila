'''''Mind''''' ist eine bedeutende britische philosophische Fachzeitschrift, die bei analytischen Tradition widmet.

Mind wurde 1876 mit George Croom Robertson durch Alexander Bain gegründet, welche die Zeitschrift am University College London herausgaben. Nach dem Tode Robertsons 1891 übernahm George Stout die Herausgabe und begann die ''New Series''. Derzeitiger Herausgeber ist Thomas Baldwin von der University of York.

Die ersten Hefte widmeten sich vor allem der Diskussion, ob die Psychologie berechtigterweise als Naturwissenschaft gelten könne.<ref>In der ersten Ausgabe schrieb Robertson: "Now, if there were a journal that set itself to record all advances in psychology, and gave encouragement to special researches by its readiness to publish them, the uncertainty hanging over the subject could hardly fail to be dispelled. Either psychology would in time pass with general consent into the company of the sciences, or the hollowness of its pretensions would be plainly revealed. Nothing less, in fact, is aimed at in the publication of Mind than to procure a decision of this question as to the scientific standing of psychology.", [http://fair-use.org/mind/1876/01/prefatory-words#e1p4 "Prefatory Words," Mind, 1 (1): 1876, p. 3]</ref>

Bereits die ersten Ausgaben von Mind publizierten bedeutende Aufsätze der sich herausbildenden analytischen Tradition, darunter etwa Bertrand Russells berühmte Abhandlung ''On Denoting'' von 1905; später erschien z.B. Alan Turings Vorschlag des Turing-Tests in der Ausgabe von 1950.<ref>[http://www.abelard.org/turpap/turpap.htm]</ref>

'''''Mind''''' is a well-respected British journal, currently published by analytic tradition. It was founded by Professor Thomas Baldwin of the University of York.

Although the journal now focuses on analytic philosophy, it began as a journal dedicated to the question of whether psychology could be a legitimate natural science. In the first issue, Robertson wrote:

"Now, if there were a journal that set itself to record all advances in psychology, and gave encouragement to special researches by its readiness to publish them, the uncertainty hanging over the subject could hardly fail to be dispelled. Either psychology would in time pass with general consent into the company of the sciences, or the hollowness of its pretensions would be plainly revealed. Nothing less, in fact, is aimed at in the publication of Mind than to procure a decision of this question as to the scientific standing of psychology."<ref>Robertson, [http://fair-use.org/mind/1876/01/prefatory-words#e1p4 "Prefatory Words," Mind, 1 (1): 1876, p. 3]; quoted at Alexander Klein, ''The Rise of Empiricism: William James, Thomas Hill Green, and the Struggle over Psychology,'' page 92 [http://individual.utoronto.ca/alex_klein/Scholarship.htm]</ref>

Bertrand Russell's famous essay: ''On Denoting'', was first published in a 1905 issue of ''Mind''.

Alan Turing first proposed the Turing test in a 1950 issue of ''Mind''.[http://www.abelard.org/turpap/turpap.htm]
