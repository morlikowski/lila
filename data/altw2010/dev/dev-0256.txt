The '''2000 Summer Olympics medal count''' is a list of nations (represented by four individual athletes from East Timor) competed in 300 events in 28 sports.<ref name=olympicsorg></ref> 

Athletes from 80&nbsp;countries won at least one medal, leaving 119&nbsp;countries without a medal. The United States won the most medals overall with 92, as well as the most gold (37) and bronze (31) medals, while Russia won the most silver medals (28). Host nation Australia finished the Games with 58 medals overall (16&nbsp;gold, 25&nbsp;silver, and 17&nbsp;bronze).<ref name=medalcount></ref> Colombia won a gold medal for the first time in its Olympic history, while Vietnam won its first ever Olympic medal, a silver in taekwondo.<ref name=olympicdyk></ref>

==Changes in medal standings==
Since the closing of these Games, the medal standings suffered changes following a doping scandal involving five-time Olympic medal winner sprinter was stripped of her three gold and two bronze medals, by the International Olympic Committee, after confessing, in October 5 2007, she had taken the anabolic steroid tetrahydrogestrinone before competing in Sydney. Along with the other four medals, the women's 100 metres gold medal has not yet been reallocated, because the presumed recipient, Ekaterini Thanou of Greece, was given a two-year ban for missing a drug test just before the 2004 Summer Olympics, in Athens.<ref></ref><ref></ref><ref></ref>
